- What is the benefit of fixed-sized pages in regards to fragmentation?

having the sizes of the split up segments all be the same size makes it so that the space does not become fragmented by ensuring space is evenly divided and not disproportionally wasting space
    
- What are some of the advantages that base/bounds has over paging (as described in this chapter)?

pages are much larger and can be a little slower to map and unmap
    
- What are some of the advantages that paging (as described in this chapter) has over base/bounds?

avoid fragmentation, and does not need to be in consecutive free space in physical memory
